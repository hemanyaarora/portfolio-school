About Me

My name is Hemanya Arora. I am an energetic programmer who is passionate about working with data and has a knack for Machine Learning. I enjoy creating insights by analyzing datasets and delivering solutions to real-world problems. My goal is to work towards building data-intensive applications and overcoming complex architectural and scalability issues in diverse industries in the future. I am also a big-time foodie. In my free time, I like lifting weights and find peace in cooking for my friends and family. Talking about my background, I come from a city named Amritsar in Punjab, India. Growing up in Amritsar was always a ride, from the fast-paced city life to the calm and peaceful weekend getaways. Nonetheless growing up in such a versatile and full-of-life city had a major role in shaping the individual I am today. After the end of my senior year of high school, I started applying to universities in Canada. After careful considerations and a month-long process, I decided to enroll into York University into the Lassonde Engineering Program given the fact that I always had an affinity towards building code-based logical functions and enrolling into Computer Science was the decision I took. Enrolling in this major gave me the confidence to explore and choose the career path to become a future Data Scientist. But taking it all the way back, to move all the way from a different country to Canada, there’s no denying the fact that everyone needs a role model in their lives who gives them direction in not only adapting to such life altering decisions, but also gives them the purpose and direction in life to follow. For me that role was filled by my dad, a hardworking individual who dedicated all his life to see his sons reach for the skies and achieve everything they ever longed for. His patience and discipline are one of the many reasons why I admire him till this day.

My Vision as an Engineer

There’s a great quote I always resonate with from Isaac Asimov, which goes as- "Science can amuse and fascinate us all, but it is engineering that changes the world." I stand by this quote as it gives me that instant drive to go out there and make a difference in the real world. But as a future engineer, my goal is to work towards a vision which will not only help me carve the path to reach my destination but also, will contribute to the betterment of my fellow engineers and society. As a person who thrives to work with data and AI, my vision for the future is to promote research and ensure ways in which we can make AI systems morally responsible. As a person who is passionate about working with AI, one of the biggest challenges the industry currently faces is making AI a much more responsible asset. Whether it’s the mechatronics industry or national defence, making AI morally responsible has become a greater matter in recent years. Just imagine, the wonders and things the human mankind will be able to achieve if we are successful in this regard. Taking a logical approach, if we are able to achieve morally responsible systems by blending a top-down explicitly coded or bottom-up adaptive AI model building we will be able to do things which never would have been possible before in the real world. For instance, a lot of decision-making processes in the world of data-intensive applications will be at ease since AI would be able to handle such at-expense decisions. Another side of the matter is the inculcation of AI systems in matters of national defence and security will not be a dream anymore. Hence, to conclude, my vision is to be a part of this wave such that my contributions to this cause can turn into a reality where AI rather than being a curse, could be a blessing in disguise so that we can make our world a better place to live in.
